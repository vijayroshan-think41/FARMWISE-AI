# Maize Pest and Disease Guide

## Overview
Maize is grown as a Kharif crop in Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, Maharashtra, and Punjab and as a spring crop in Punjab and UP. The introduction of Fall Armyworm (Spodoptera frugiperda) in 2018 has become a major new threat to maize cultivation across India. Early detection and management of this pest is now a top priority.

## Pest and Disease Entries

---

### Fall Armyworm (Spodoptera frugiperda)

**Type:** Insect (Lepidoptera) — Invasive Species

**Symptoms:**
- Window-pane feeding on young leaves — transparent patches
- Ragged leaf damage with sawdust-like frass in whorl
- Pinholes on leaves arranged in rows
- In severe cases, entire whorl is consumed — dead heart at vegetative stage
- Caterpillar has inverted Y mark on head; four square spots on abdomen tip

**Triggering Conditions:**
- Temperature: 25–35°C
- Season: Active throughout Kharif; peak at whorl stage (V4–V8)
- Migrates over long distances — entire regions affected simultaneously
- Highly resistant to many pesticides — rotate chemicals

**Treatment:**
- Primary chemical: Chlorantraniliprole (Coragen) 18.5% SC — 0.3 ml/litre into whorl
- Alternatively: Spinetoram 11.7% SC — 0.9 ml/litre
- Application method: Apply directly into whorl using knapsack — cover all plants

**Organic Alternative:**
- Bacillus thuringiensis var. kurstaki — 2 g/litre into whorl at early larval stage
- NSKE 5% spray every 5 days
- Release Trichogramma pretiosum egg parasitoid cards

**Prevention:**
- Monitor from emergence — check 20 plants per acre twice weekly
- Install pheromone traps for Fall Armyworm — 5 per acre
- Early planting to avoid peak moth migration period
- Encourage natural enemies — do not spray broad-spectrum insecticides

### Maize Streak Virus (MSV)

**Type:** Viral

**Symptoms:**
- Bright yellow to white streaks running parallel to leaf veins
- Young infected plants are severely stunted
- No grain set in infected plants
- Transmitted by leafhoppers (Cicadulina spp.)
- No cure once infected

**Triggering Conditions:**
- Leafhopper populations high in warm dry conditions
- Season: Early Kharif when leafhoppers are active
- Young plants (V1–V6) are most susceptible

**Treatment:**
- No treatment — infected plants must be removed and destroyed
- Control leafhopper vector with Imidacloprid 17.8% SL — 0.5 ml/litre

**Organic Alternative:**
- Reflective silver mulch repels leafhoppers
- Neem oil spray on young plants to deter leafhoppers

**Prevention:**
- Use resistant hybrid varieties
- Seed treatment with Imidacloprid to protect against early leafhopper feeding
- Roguing and destroying infected plants immediately

### Stalk Rot (Fusarium moniliforme & Pythium spp.)

**Type:** Fungal

**Symptoms:**
- Lower internodes soften and rot — stalk collapses
- Internal stalk tissue shows pinkish or brown discoloration
- Plant lodges before maturity — ear contacts ground and rots
- Roots become brown and necrotic
- Premature ripening with poor grain fill

**Triggering Conditions:**
- High nitrogen, low potassium nutrition
- Water stress followed by heavy rain at tasseling
- Season: August–September in Kharif
- Dense planting reduces air circulation

**Treatment:**
- No effective field treatment — focus on prevention
- Harvest early if stalk rot detected to minimize loss

**Organic Alternative:**
- Trichoderma viride seed treatment
- Balanced potassium application reduces susceptibility

**Prevention:**
- Balanced NPK — do not skip potassium
- Avoid water stress at tasseling and silking
- Plant at recommended spacing — do not crowd
- Use stalk rot resistant hybrids
- Harvest promptly at maturity — delayed harvest worsens loss

### Downy Mildew (Peronosclerospora sorghi)

**Type:** Fungal (Oomycete)

**Symptoms:**
- White downy growth on underside of lower leaves
- Pale green to yellow striping on upper leaf surface
- Severely affected plants are stunted with narrow twisted leaves
- No ears formed — or small deformed ears
- Systemic infection — entire plant affected from seed or early infection

**Triggering Conditions:**
- Cool humid conditions
- High rainfall at germination stage
- Season: Early Kharif — June to July

**Treatment:**
- Primary chemical: Metalaxyl 35% WS seed treatment — 6 g/kg seed before sowing
- Foliar spray with Metalaxyl + Mancozeb — 2.5 g/litre at first symptom

**Organic Alternative:**
- No effective organic treatment at advanced stage
- Remove and destroy infected plants before sporulation

**Prevention:**
- Seed treatment with Metalaxyl is essential in downy mildew-prone areas
- Use resistant hybrids
- Avoid planting in waterlogged or low-lying areas
- Crop rotation with non-host crops (legumes, cotton)
