# Mustard Pest and Disease Guide

## Overview
Mustard is the primary oilseed Rabi crop of Rajasthan, Haryana, Madhya Pradesh, and Uttar Pradesh. Aphids are the most economically damaging pest, capable of causing 40–60% yield loss in severe infestations. Timely monitoring and action at the economic threshold level is critical for profitable mustard cultivation.

## Pest and Disease Entries

---

### Alternaria Blight (Alternaria brassicae)

**Type:** Fungal

**Symptoms:**
- Circular brown to dark brown spots with concentric rings on leaves
- Yellow halo around spots; older spots merge to form large blighted areas
- Dark elongated lesions on stems and branches
- Silique (pod) infection causes premature shattering and seed discoloration
- Defoliation under severe infection

**Triggering Conditions:**
- Temperature: 20–28°C
- Humidity: Above 75%; dew and fog in Rabi season
- Season: January to March in Rajasthan, Haryana

**Treatment:**
- Primary chemical: Mancozeb 75% WP — 2 g/litre spray every 10 days
- Alternatively: Iprodione 50% WP — 1.5 g/litre at pod formation stage

**Organic Alternative:**
- Copper oxychloride 3 g/litre spray
- Neem oil 5 ml/litre fortnightly

**Prevention:**
- Use certified disease-free seed
- Seed treatment with Thiram 75% WS — 3 g/kg seed
- Maintain spacing of 45×15 cm for air circulation
- Avoid overhead irrigation — use drip or furrow

### White Rust (Albugo candida)

**Type:** Fungal (Oomycete)

**Symptoms:**
- White blister-like pustules on underside of leaves, stems, and inflorescences
- Upper leaf surface shows yellow patches opposite pustules
- Infected flower stalks become distorted and swollen (staghead)
- Staghead causes complete failure of pod formation
- Can cause 30–40% yield loss in severe years

**Triggering Conditions:**
- Temperature: 10–18°C — cool weather
- Humidity: High; fog and dew in December–January
- Season: December to February

**Treatment:**
- Primary chemical: Metalaxyl + Mancozeb — 2.5 g/litre spray at first sign
- Alternatively: Ridomil Gold 2.5 g/litre

**Organic Alternative:**
- Bordeaux mixture 1% spray
- Remove and destroy infected flower stalks immediately

**Prevention:**
- Use white rust tolerant varieties (Pusa Bold, Kranti)
- Timely sowing — October 1–15
- Crop rotation with non-crucifer crops

### Aphids (Lipaphis erysimi — Mustard Aphid)

**Type:** Insect

**Symptoms:**
- Dense colonies of mustard-green aphids on growing tips, flowers, and pods
- Leaves and shoots curl inward and distort
- Honeydew causes sooty mold
- Flowering and pod set severely disrupted — main yield loss cause
- Economic threshold: 20–25 aphids per plant at vegetative; 46–50 per plant at flowering

**Triggering Conditions:**
- Temperature: 15–25°C; cold dry January conditions are ideal
- Season: January to February — peak in cold dry spells
- Populations can increase 10x in a week during favorable conditions

**Treatment:**
- Primary chemical: Dimethoate 30% EC (Rogor) — 2 ml/litre spray at economic threshold
- Alternatively: Oxydemeton methyl (Metasystox) — 1.5 ml/litre
- Application method: Spray in morning; cover all plant parts including flower clusters

**Organic Alternative:**
- Neem oil 5 ml/litre + soap 2 ml/litre spray
- Spray high-pressure water jet to dislodge colonies
- Protect natural enemies — syrphid flies, ladybirds, lacewings

**Prevention:**
- Timely sowing reduces aphid exposure period
- Avoid late sowing which extends crop into peak aphid season
- Monitor twice weekly from December
- Attract natural enemies by maintaining habitat diversity

### Sawfly (Athalia proxima)

**Type:** Insect (Hymenoptera)

**Symptoms:**
- Caterpillar-like larvae (slate-gray with orange-yellow tinge) feeding on leaves
- Leaves skeletonized — only veins remain
- Severe attack can defoliate entire field rapidly
- Adults are yellow and black wasp-like insects
- Early stage (October–November) attack on seedlings most damaging

**Triggering Conditions:**
- Season: October to November after sowing; second generation in February
- Cool moist conditions after first rain following sowing
- More severe in late-sown crops

**Treatment:**
- Primary chemical: Quinalphos 25% EC — 2 ml/litre spray
- Alternatively: Malathion 50% EC — 2 ml/litre

**Organic Alternative:**
- Neem oil 5 ml/litre spray
- Hand collection of larvae in small fields
- Spray in evening when larvae are active

**Prevention:**
- Timely sowing — October 1–15
- Early sowing escapes first generation peak
- Deep plowing to destroy pupae in soil
- Maintain field hygiene
