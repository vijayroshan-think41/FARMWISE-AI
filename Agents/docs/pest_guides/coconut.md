# Coconut Pest and Disease Guide

## Overview
Coconut is Kerala's most important crop and a major source of livelihood for millions of farmers in coastal India. Root wilt disease is an endemic and incurable problem in certain zones of Kerala, while rhinoceros beetle and red palm weevil cause serious damage to palms across India. Integrated management combining regular monitoring, cultural practices, and targeted treatments is essential.

---

## Pest and Disease Entries

---

### Root Wilt Disease (Phytoplasma — suspected)

**Type:** Phytoplasma (Mycoplasma-like organism)

**Symptoms:**
- Leaf flaccidity — leaves hang down losing their normal erect posture
- Yellowing starts from outer older leaves and progresses inward
- Root necrosis — feeder roots turn brown and die
- Reduced nut production; nuts smaller with less kernel
- Eventual death of palm over years
- No cure exists — incurable endemic disease in certain zones of Kerala

**Triggering Conditions:**
- Endemic in certain areas of Kerala (Kuttanad, Kochi region)
- Spreads through planthoppers (Proutista moesta) which transmit phytoplasma
- Poorly drained, waterlogged soils worsen symptoms
- Any season; progressive disease

**Treatment:**
- No cure — palliative care to extend productive life of affected palms
- Inject micronutrient mixture (ferrous sulfate, zinc sulfate, manganese sulfate) into trunk
- Drench root zone with dilute NPK fertilizer
- Keep affected palms well-nourished to maintain partial productivity

**Organic Alternative:**
- Organic matter application improves root health and slows progression
- Vermicompost 25 kg per palm annually

**Prevention:**
- Plant certified Root Wilt-resistant varieties in endemic areas
- Control planthopper vector with Imidacloprid drench
- Improve drainage in root zone
- Do not plant coconut in areas with known root wilt history

### Bud Rot (Phytophthora palmivora)

**Type:** Fungal (Oomycete)

**Symptoms:**
- Yellowing and wilting of innermost spear leaf (spindle)
- Spindle pulls out easily — base shows dark brown rotting with foul smell
- Rotting progresses down into growing point (bud) — killing the palm
- Coconut has a single growing point — once bud dies, palm cannot recover
- Outer leaves remain green initially making early detection difficult

**Triggering Conditions:**
- Heavy rainfall during monsoon — especially June–August
- Young palms (1–5 years) most vulnerable
- Waterlogged poorly-drained soils
- High humidity with poor air circulation in dense plantings

**Treatment:**
- Remove rotted spindle and bud tissue completely — clean with knife
- Fill cavity with Bordeaux paste (copper sulfate + lime)
- Pour Copper oxychloride 3 g/litre solution into bud cavity
- For early-stage: Metalaxyl drench at vine base

**Organic Alternative:**
- Bordeaux paste applied to exposed tissue after removal of infected bud
- Trichoderma application to root zone as preventive

**Prevention:**
- Drain excess water from root zone
- Remove and destroy infected spindles immediately
- Spray Bordeaux mixture 1% on crown and collar before monsoon
- Avoid injuries to palm — beetles create entry points for Phytophthora

### Rhinoceros Beetle (Oryctes rhinoceros)

**Type:** Insect (Coleoptera)

**Symptoms:**
- V-shaped or rectangular cuts on fronds — characteristic feeding damage
- Holes bored into soft central shoot (crown)
- Cut fronds hang like flag leaves
- Bud damage causes deformed leaves with serrated edges
- Severe crown boring kills the growing point and eventually the palm
- Grubs breed in decaying organic matter — compost heaps, log piles

**Triggering Conditions:**
- Adults most active at night — breed in decaying wood and compost
- New plantings near decaying coconut logs are at high risk
- Season: Year-round in tropical humid climate

**Treatment:**
- Remove adult beetles from crown — use hooked wire inserted into bore hole
- Apply Naphthalene balls (3–4 per crown) to repel adults
- Carbaryl 5% dust — 25 g applied into bore hole

**Organic Alternative:**
- Oryctes baculovirus (Nudivirus) biocontrol — mix in breeding sites
- Apply to all identified breeding sites
- Metarhizium anisopliae fungus application in compost heaps

**Prevention:**
- Remove and burn all decaying organic matter — coconut stumps, compost heaps
- Do not allow coconut logs to decompose near garden
- Install pheromone traps — 1 per 5 acres to mass-trap adults
- Inject Neem oil into bore holes

### Red Palm Weevil (Rhynchophorus ferrugineus)

**Type:** Insect (Coleoptera)

**Symptoms:**
- Large bore holes in trunk near crown with brown fibrous material extruding
- Fermented odor from bore site
- Wilting and yellowing of crown leaves
- Tunneling damage throughout trunk interior
- Palm dies once the central growing point is reached
- Grubs (large white C-shaped) found inside trunk — up to 60 per palm

**Triggering Conditions:**
- Entry through wounds — beetle attack follows rhinoceros beetle damage, pruning cuts, or injuries
- Any season; year-round in coastal humid areas
- Pheromone attractant produced by infested palms draws more beetles

**Treatment:**
- Inject Chlorpyrifos 20% EC — 5 ml diluted in 100 ml water into bore holes
- Apply cotton wicks soaked in insecticide into holes and seal with clay
- For early detection: insert probe into soft crown area to check for tunneling

**Organic Alternative:**
- Entomopathogenic nematodes (Steinernema carpocapsae) injected into bore holes
- Pheromone traps for mass trapping adults

**Prevention:**
- Prevent all trunk injuries — rhinoceros beetle wounds are primary entry points
- Install pheromone traps — Ferrolure+ pheromone lure, 1 trap per 5 acres
- Apply Carbaryl paste on all pruning cuts and wounds immediately
- Remove severely infested palms completely — burn trunk on site
- Regular monitoring — inspect crown monthly
