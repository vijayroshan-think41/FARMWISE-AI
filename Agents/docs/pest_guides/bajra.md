# Bajra (Pearl Millet) Pest and Disease Guide

## Overview
Bajra is the primary dryland cereal of Rajasthan, Gujarat, and Haryana. It is highly drought-tolerant but susceptible to downy mildew and ergot under specific conditions. It is the staple food crop for millions of small farmers in arid regions where no other cereal can be reliably grown.

## Pest and Disease Entries

---

### Downy Mildew (Green Ear Disease — Sclerospora graminicola)

**Type:** Fungal (Oomycete)

**Symptoms:**
- Systemic infection — plants stunted and pale from emergence
- White downy growth on lower leaf surface
- Panicle (ear) converted into leaf-like structures (green ear) — no grain formed
- Infected plants are called witches broom — multiple tillers, none forming grain
- Oospores in soil persist for 10+ years

**Triggering Conditions:**
- Cool humid conditions at germination (below 30°C, high humidity)
- Season: Early Kharif — June to July
- Waterlogged or poorly drained soils
- Seed-borne and soil-borne

**Treatment:**
- No field treatment for systemically infected plants — rogue and destroy
- Metalaxyl 35% WS seed treatment — 6 g/kg seed prevents seed-borne infection

**Organic Alternative:**
- Hot water seed treatment — 55°C for 10 minutes
- Use downy mildew-resistant varieties

**Prevention:**
- Use certified seed from downy mildew-free areas
- Metalaxyl seed treatment mandatory in endemic areas
- Remove green ear plants before sporulation
- Crop rotation — avoid pearl millet after pearl millet

### Ergot (Claviceps fusiformis)

**Type:** Fungal

**Symptoms:**
- Honeydew (sticky liquid) oozing from florets at flowering stage
- Honeydew is the first sign — attracts flies and bees
- Honeydew replaced by hard dark purple-brown sclerotia (ergot bodies)
- Ergot bodies replace individual grains in panicle
- Ergot contains toxic alkaloids — grain is unfit for human or animal consumption

**Triggering Conditions:**
- Cool humid conditions at flowering (below 20°C, high humidity)
- High altitude bajra fields (above 500 m) in Rajasthan
- Season: August when early-sown bajra flowers during cooler humid conditions

**Treatment:**
- No chemical treatment — prevention only
- Remove and destroy infected panicles before sclerotia mature

**Organic Alternative:**
- Wash panicles with water spray to reduce honeydew and spore spread

**Prevention:**
- Sow recommended varieties on time — avoid early sowing in high-altitude areas
- Use ergot-resistant or tolerant varieties
- Do not use infected grain as seed
- Immerse seed in 10% salt solution — ergot bodies float and can be removed

### Shoot Fly (Atherigona approximata)

**Type:** Insect (Diptera)

**Symptoms:**
- Dead heart in seedlings — central shoot pulls out easily with foul smell
- Yellowing and wilting of central leaf before death
- Maggots visible inside killed shoot
- Severely attacked fields require re-sowing
- Economic threshold: 10% dead hearts

**Triggering Conditions:**
- Temperature: 25–35°C
- Humid conditions at seedling stage
- Season: Early Kharif — June to July
- Late-sown bajra escapes shoot fly as pest population declines

**Treatment:**
- Primary chemical: Chlorpyrifos 20% EC — 2 ml/litre foliar spray at 10–15 DAS
- Alternatively: Thiamethoxam 25% WG — 0.3 g/litre spray

**Organic Alternative:**
- Neem seed kernel extract 5% spray
- Seed treatment with Imidacloprid 600 FS — 5 ml/kg

**Prevention:**
- Timely sowing — June 15 to July 15 is optimum
- Seed treatment with Imidacloprid 600 FS
- Use resistant varieties (MPMH-17, HHB-67 Improved)
- Destroy crop debris from previous season

### Stem Borer (Chilo partellus)

**Type:** Insect (Lepidoptera)

**Symptoms:**
- Dead heart at vegetative stage — similar to shoot fly but older plants
- Pinholes on leaves from early larval feeding
- Frass visible in whorl
- Late infestation causes chaffy panicles and broken stems

**Triggering Conditions:**
- Temperature: 25–35°C
- Season: July to August — after shoot fly peak
- Humid conditions

**Treatment:**
- Primary chemical: Fipronil 0.3% GR — 7.5 kg/ha applied in whorl
- Alternatively: Chlorantraniliprole 18.5% SC — 0.3 ml/litre spray

**Organic Alternative:**
- Trichogramma chilonis egg cards — 5 cards per acre
- Bacillus thuringiensis spray — 2 g/litre

**Prevention:**
- Early sowing to avoid peak borer season
- Light traps to catch adult moths
- Intercrop with cowpea which harbors natural enemies
