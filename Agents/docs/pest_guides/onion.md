# Onion Pest and Disease Guide

## Overview
Onion is a high-value commercial crop grown extensively in Maharashtra (Nashik), Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, and Madhya Pradesh. It is highly sensitive to thrips and fungal diseases during bulb development. The crop requires careful monitoring as late-stage disease or pest damage directly impacts market quality and storability.

---

## Pest and Disease Entries

---

### Purple Blotch (Alternaria porri)

**Type:** Fungal

**Symptoms:**
- Small white lesions with purple center on leaves — turns into large papery patches
- Lesions have water-soaked margins, eventually girdling the leaf
- Infected leaves collapse and dry — reducing photosynthesis and bulb fill
- Disease progresses from older outer leaves inward
- Infected seed stalks break at lesion sites

**Triggering Conditions:**
- Temperature: 21–30°C
- Humidity: Above 80%; dew and fog favor infection
- Season: Kharif October–November; Rabi February–March
- Wounded leaves from thrips feeding are primary entry points

**Treatment:**
- Primary chemical: Mancozeb 75% WP — 2 g/litre spray every 7 days
- Alternatively: Iprodione 50% WP — 1.5 g/litre
- Application method: Foliar spray with sticker (Triton X-100 0.1%) on waxy leaves

**Organic Alternative:**
- Copper oxychloride 3 g/litre spray
- Garlic extract 5% spray has antifungal properties

**Prevention:**
- Use certified disease-free seed or treated seed
- Avoid overhead irrigation — use drip
- Maintain spacing of 10×10 cm for air circulation
- Rotate with non-allium crops every 2–3 years
- Apply sticker to improve spray adhesion on waxy leaf surface

### Thrips (Thrips tabaci — Onion Thrips)

**Type:** Insect (Thysanoptera)

**Symptoms:**
- Silver streaks and white patches on inner leaves (feeding scars)
- Leaves curl inward; tips dry and turn brown
- Tiny yellow insects visible with magnification inside leaf whorls
- Severely infested plants are stunted with malformed bulbs
- Thrips transmit Iris Yellow Spot Virus (IYSV)
- Economic threshold: 15–20 thrips per plant

**Triggering Conditions:**
- Temperature: 25–35°C; hot dry weather causes population explosion
- Season: Rabi October–March peak; Kharif also significant
- Dry dusty conditions favor thrips
- Dense planting traps heat and creates microhabitat

**Treatment:**
- Primary chemical: Spinosad 45% SC — 0.3 ml/litre spray targeting leaf base
- Alternatively: Fipronil 5% SC — 1.5 ml/litre; rotate with spinosad to prevent resistance
- Application method: Spray into leaf whorl and base — not just on leaf surface

**Organic Alternative:**
- Neem oil 5 ml/litre + soap 2 ml/litre — spray every 5 days
- Blue sticky traps — 10 per acre to monitor and mass-trap
- Spray in morning or evening when thrips are less active

**Prevention:**
- Irrigate frequently — thrips dislike wet conditions
- Use reflective silver mulch to disorient thrips
- Avoid planting near older infested crops
- Remove and destroy crop debris immediately after harvest
- Plant marigold border rows as trap crop

### Downy Mildew (Peronospora destructor)

**Type:** Fungal (Oomycete)

**Symptoms:**
- Pale green to yellow patches on outer leaves, covered with violet-gray fuzzy growth in morning
- Infected leaves collapse and dry from tip downward
- Plants in affected patches appear pale and water-stressed
- Bulbs from infected plants store poorly and rot quickly

**Triggering Conditions:**
- Temperature: 13–25°C (cool humid conditions)
- Humidity: Very high; dew critical for spore germination
- Season: November–February Rabi season during foggy cool nights

**Treatment:**
- Primary chemical: Metalaxyl + Mancozeb (Ridomil Gold) — 2.5 g/litre spray
- Application method: Foliar spray every 10 days; use sticker for adhesion on waxy leaves

**Organic Alternative:**
- Copper-based fungicides (Bordeaux 1%) spray every 7–10 days
- Improve drainage and air circulation

**Prevention:**
- Use disease-free seed and sets
- Adequate spacing — 15×10 cm minimum
- Avoid evening irrigation which promotes overnight leaf wetness
- Crop rotation with non-allium crops

### Basal Rot (Fusarium oxysporum f. sp. cepae)

**Type:** Fungal

**Symptoms:**
- Plants wilt progressively despite adequate moisture
- Outer leaves yellow from tips downward
- Pinkish-red rot at base of bulb (basal plate)
- Roots show brown discoloration and rot
- Infected bulbs collapse in storage — entire storage lot can be lost

**Triggering Conditions:**
- Soil temperature: 20–28°C
- Favored by waterlogged soils and poor drainage
- Season: Any season but worse in Kharif due to heavy rain
- Spreads in soil — persists for 8–10 years

**Treatment:**
- Primary chemical: Carbendazim 50% WP — drench at 1 g/litre around affected plants
- No effective cure for advanced infection — remove affected plants

**Organic Alternative:**
- Trichoderma viride — soil application 2.5 kg/ha at transplanting
- Pseudomonas fluorescens drench around plant base

**Prevention:**
- Seed and set treatment with Thiram 75% WS — 3 g/kg
- Raise transplants in sterilized soil
- Improve field drainage — ridge and furrow planting
- Crop rotation — avoid allium crops for 3–4 years in infected field
- Avoid waterlogging at any growth stage
