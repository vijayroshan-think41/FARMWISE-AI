Regional Notes

Rainfall Pattern
Tamil Nadu receives rainfall primarily from the Northeast Monsoon (October–December). The Cauvery Delta region gets
900–1200 mm annually. Kharif season relies on Southwest Monsoon which is moderate (June–September). Tank and canal
irrigation supplements rain-fed farming.

Common Challenges
Blast disease in rice during humid conditions. Tikka leaf spot in groundnut during Kharif. Sugarcane internode borer active
June–August. Drought stress in Rabi groundnut if tank levels are low. Saline soil patches near coastal areas reduce rice
yield.

Intercropping Recommendations
Groundnut + Castor (2:1 ratio) for Kharif season. Rice + Azolla as green manure improves nitrogen fixation. Sugarcane +
Onion during early growth period (first 60 days). Groundnut + Sorghum border rows to repel aphids.

Government Advisory
Use Kuruvai rice varieties (ADT-39, CO-51) for June sowing. Adopt System of Rice Intensification (SRI) to reduce water use
by 30%. Tamil Nadu Agriculture Department recommends soil testing every 3 years.
