Regional Notes
Rainfall Pattern
Nashik and Deccan Plateau receive 600–800 mm rainfall annually from the Southwest Monsoon (June–September). Rain is
concentrated in July–August. October onwards is dry. Borewell irrigation is essential for Rabi season. Black cotton soil
retains moisture well but cracks in dry season.

Common Challenges
Late blight in tomato during October–November when humidity rises. Thrips infestation in onion during dry Rabi season.
Wheat rust (yellow and brown) in February–March when temperatures rise. Water scarcity in March–May affects summer
crops. Borewell dependency increases cost of cultivation.

Intercropping Recommendations
Tomato + Basil repels whitefly and improves aroma. Onion + Carrot improves land utilization in Rabi. Wheat + Chickpea at
4:1 ratio for Rabi season. Marigold as border crop around tomato to trap fruit borer.

Market Notes
Nashik is India's largest onion market. Onion prices are highly volatile — monitor APMC Lasalgaon daily. Tomato prices peak
in January–February. Wheat procurement at MSP through FCI is available. Plan harvest timing to avoid market glut in
October for Kharif onion.
