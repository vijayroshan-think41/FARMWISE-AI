Regional Notes
Rainfall Pattern
Rajasthan receives only 200–400 mm of annual rainfall, concentrated in July–August from the Southwest Monsoon. Western
Rajasthan (Jodhpur, Barmer) receives less than 200 mm. Jaipur district averages 550 mm. Hot dry winds (loo) in May–June
cause crop damage. Fog in December–January affects mustard and cumin.

Common Challenges
Water scarcity is the primary constraint — every crop choice must factor water availability. Downy mildew in bajra during
humid spells after rain. Aphids in mustard are the most serious pest — they can cause 40–50% yield loss if uncontrolled.
Cumin wilt (Fusarium) spreads in waterlogged conditions — never over-irrigate cumin. Sandy soil has low nutrient retention
— apply organic matter regularly.

Intercropping Recommendations
Bajra + Moth Bean (2:1) — traditional and drought-tolerant system. Mustard + Chickpea (2:1) in Rabi for risk management.
Cumin is always grown alone — intercropping reduces essential oil quality. Cluster bean + Bajra mixed cropping for fodder
and grain.

Water Conservation Advisory
Rajasthan Government provides drip irrigation subsidy up to 70% for horticulture crops. Rainwater harvesting through tanka
and kund systems recommended. Micro watershed structures help recharge groundwater. Indira Gandhi Canal commands
area in western Rajasthan — farmers here can grow more water-intensive crops. Mulching with crop residue reduces
evaporation by 30%.
