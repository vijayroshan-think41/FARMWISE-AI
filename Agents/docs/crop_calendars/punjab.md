Regional Notes
Rainfall Pattern
Punjab receives 600–900 mm annually, mostly from the Southwest Monsoon (July–September). Winters are cold and foggy
(December–January) which impacts wheat tillering. Summer is very hot (May–June). Canal irrigation from Beas and Sutlej
rivers is well developed. Groundwater is at risk of depletion from paddy cultivation.

Common Challenges
Yellow rust is the most serious wheat disease in Punjab — monitor from January. Rice stubble burning causes air pollution
and depletes soil organic matter. Brown plant hopper in rice has developed insecticide resistance — use IPM. Waterlogging
in low-lying areas during heavy monsoon. High water use in paddy — Punjab Government promotes Direct Seeded Rice
(DSR).

Intercropping Recommendations
Wheat + Mustard border rows (6:1) in Rabi. Maize + Cowpea (3:1) improves soil nitrogen. Basmati rice is grown as mono
crop — do not intercrop. Consider maize-wheat rotation instead of rice-wheat to reduce water use.

Water Advisory
Punjab State Government offers subsidy for drip and sprinkler irrigation. Direct Seeded Rice (DSR) saves 15–20% water vs
transplanted paddy. Avoid paddy sowing before June 10 (state law) to reduce groundwater extraction. Laser land leveling
improves irrigation efficiency by 20%.
