Regional Notes
Rainfall Pattern

Kerala receives over 3000 mm of annual rainfall from two monsoons — Southwest (June–September) and Northeast
(October–November). Dry season is December–May. Thrissur district averages 2800–3200 mm annually. Excess rain
causes waterlogging and fungal diseases. Drainage management is as important as irrigation.

Common Challenges
Phytophthora foot rot (quick wilt) in black pepper is a major economic threat — treated with Bordeaux mixture. Coconut root
wilt disease is endemic in certain areas. Rhinoceros beetle damages young coconut palms. Banana bunchy top virus
spreads through aphids — use virus-free planting material. Rubber requires 5–7 years before tapping begins.

Intercropping Recommendations
Coconut + Black Pepper (grown up coconut palm as standard). Coconut + Banana + Cocoa in multi-tier system. Coconut +
Pineapple ground cover. Rubber + Cocoa in young rubber plantations (first 5 years). Tapioca + Cowpea in mixed planting.

Organic Farming Advisory
Kerala promotes organic farming for spices and coconut. Use Trichoderma viride for soil treatment. Green manure crops like
sun hemp improve soil health. Vermicompost application recommended for all crops. Neem cake (2 kg/palm) annually for
coconut improves resistance to root wilt.
